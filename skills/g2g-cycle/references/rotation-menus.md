# Rotation menus — theoretical framework families by discipline

Purpose: enforce R1 (no two consecutive cycles in the same family). These are defaults; the user edits their own copy in `g2g/00_programme.md` at setup. A "family" is a way of generating mechanisms, not a single model.

## Economics / finance (primary audience)

- Discrete choice and demand systems (MNL, mixed logit, BLP, non-homothetic preferences, Engel structures)
- Search and matching (labor, marriage, product markets)
- Bargaining and contracts (Nash bargaining, incomplete contracts, collective household models)
- Information economics (adverse selection, signaling, screening, moral hazard)
- Behavioral (reference dependence, present bias, mental accounting, social preferences, status/Veblen goods)
- Overlapping generations and life-cycle models
- Networks and contagion (production networks, financial contagion, peer effects)
- Political economy and state capacity (Besley–Persson, Tilly, selectorate logic)
- Growth and distribution (Kaldor facts, Piketty-style wealth dynamics, structural transformation)
- Market microstructure and frictions (price stickiness, pass-through asymmetry, menu costs)
- General equilibrium with heterogeneous agents (HANK-style logic, incidence analysis)

## Sociology

Field theory (Bourdieu: capital, habitus) · Social network analysis (ties, brokerage, diffusion) · Institutional theory (isomorphism, legitimacy) · Stratification and mobility tables · Cultural repertoires and boundary work · Life-course analysis · Organizational ecology

## Political science

Veto players and institutional constraints · Selectorate theory · Collective action (Olson, Ostrom) · Cleavage and coalition formation · Principal–agent in bureaucracies · Audience costs and signaling · State capacity and fiscal sociology

## Psychology / behavioral

Dual-process accounts · Prospect theory and framing · Social identity theory · Scarcity and cognitive bandwidth · Norm activation and nudging

## Cross-cutting (any discipline)

Evolutionary/selection arguments · Agent-based modeling · Causal-graph reasoning (DAGs, identification-first design) · Comparative-historical analysis (process tracing, natural experiments) · Formal epistemology (Bayesian updating models)

## Usage rules

- Record the family used in every registry row and conclusion card.
- If the user insists on repeating a family two cycles in a row, comply but log a rotation waiver in the contradiction log (the record keeps everyone honest).
- When a family is exhausted for a question, that is information: log which families the question has survived — robustness across families is a quality signal grounding cannot give.
